Dartpop

DartPop is a thrifting app for Dartmouth students to list and buy items (ranging from clothes to textbooks) from each other, allowing them to update their wardrobes and get rid of clothes they no longer wear during the term.

User Research

Our project began with preliminary user interviews, inquiring about people’s attitude and experience with e-commerce apps and any challenges they have. We found that their foremost problems revolved around optimizing both time and cost efficiency.

Solution Ideation

We brainstormed various solutions and created wireframes. Our aim was to cater to both students interested in selling items and those looking to purchase items from fellow students.

Design & Prototyping

We designed high-fidelity prototypes and tested them with users. Many users enjoyed the profile section being separated into three different sections with a scrollable image grid the best. They also felt the profile photo should be smaller and they wanted their reviews to not be directly under their name but rather incorporated into the image grid.

Final Product

Dartpop was created to provide Dartmouth students with a way to get the items they need fast and cheap while also making extra money and decluttering their dorm. This final project fine-tuned my problem-solving skills, user research skills, and collaboration skills.
